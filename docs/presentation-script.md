# Seawolf Connect — Presentation Script
## The Sarah Chen Story (Student Perspective)

> **Total length:** ~3 min 40 sec
> **Audience:** SBU Student Affairs
> **Format:** English script + Chinese stage directions

---

## 第 1 段 — Sarah 登场（Landing + Login）｜约 35 秒

### 操作
1. 打开 https://seawolf-connect.vercel.app 停在首页 Hero 区
2. 点击右上角 **"Sign In with SBU NetID"** 按钮 → 跳转 `/dashboard`

### 演讲稿
> "Meet **Sarah Chen**. She's a Computer Science sophomore at Stony Brook — and she's spent her **entire sophomore year second-guessing her major**.
>
> She's checking every box for graduation. Her GPA is fine. She has an academic advisor. But advising at SBU is structured and formal — it feels like a *doctor's appointment*. What Sarah actually needs is **someone who's stood where she's standing** — and lived to tell.
>
> She signs in with her **SBU NetID** — same login as Brightspace and SOLAR. No new account. No new password. **Just a familiar door** into a community of people who've already done this."

### 重点
- 第一句慢，让观众记住 Sarah 的 "sophomore + second-guessing"
- "doctor's appointment" 是 Slide 6 对比表原话，要清晰念出
- "familiar door" 落点在学生体验，不要把它讲成 DoIT 运维卖点

---

## 第 2 段 — Dashboard｜约 45 秒

### 操作
1. 停在 `/dashboard`
2. 手指指向顶部 Welcome banner（"Welcome back, Sarah!"）
3. 滚动到推荐导师卡片（4 张）
4. 滑到侧边栏的 Upcoming Appointments / Community Topics

### 演讲稿
> "This is her dashboard. The system already knows she's a sophomore CS major doubting her path — so the mentors surfaced first aren't random. They're **AI-matched** to where she is *right now*: a senior who switched out of CS and back, a Google alum who started off as lost as Sarah is, a Goldman Sachs analyst who took the long way around.
>
> Three sessions on her calendar this week. And on the right — what her community is asking. *'Best CS electives for AI/ML' — 23 replies. 'Internship timelines' — 31 replies.* Every question Sarah is afraid to ask out loud, **someone at SBU has already asked it for her**."

### 重点
- "where she is right now" + "started off as lost as Sarah is" 呼应 Slide 6 "someone who gets it"
- 三个 mentor 的描述加入**共情线索**（switched out and back / started off as lost / long way around），不是 credential 堆砌
- 最后一句是情感共鸣点，放慢语速

---

## 第 3 段 — Explore + Mentor Profile｜约 1 分 20 秒

### 操作
1. 点击顶部导航 **"Explore"**
2. 在左侧筛选器勾选 **Computer Science** + **Interview Prep**
3. 列表缩减后，点击 **Priya Patel** 的 "Connect"
4. 依次展示：Bio → Weekly Availability 日历 → Reviews
5. 手停在右侧的红色 **"Request Meeting"** 按钮

### 演讲稿
> "But Sarah doesn't have to take what the algorithm gives her. She filters by **Computer Science + Interview Prep**, and a handful of highly relevant mentors surface. Each card shows rating, session count, specialties, availability.
>
> She clicks on **Priya Patel** — a senior who interned at Deloitte and IBM. Real bio. Real expertise. Structured weekly availability — not *'DM me and we'll figure it out.'* And actual reviews from actual mentees — including this one, from Sarah herself, three months ago: ***'Got 3 interview callbacks within a week.'***
>
> Three months before that review, Sarah was **applying blindly with zero responses**. Today, she's not just a name on an application — she's a **warm introduction**. That's what mentorship gives her: not a magic answer, but **social capital** she didn't have on day one.
>
> One click — **Request Meeting** — it's scheduled. **No email tag. No scheduling Doodle.**"

### 重点
- 筛选演示最视觉化，操作**要慢**
- 指出来自 "Sarah Chen" 的那条评价 —— 剧情回环，停顿一下
- "applying blindly → warm introduction → social capital" 三段式直接来自 Slide 6 对比表
- "No email tag. No scheduling Doodle." 节奏放慢

---

## 第 4 段 — Messages + Profile｜约 40 秒

### 操作
1. 点击顶部导航 **"Messages"**，展示和 Priya/David/Ryan 的对话历史
2. 快速点击顶部导航 **"My Profile"**，展示可编辑的目标、标签、偏好设置

### 演讲稿
> "Mentorship isn't one meeting — it's an ongoing relationship. Every conversation Sarah has with Priya, with David, with Ryan — they live **inside the SBU ecosystem**. Not on Discord. Not in DMs that disappear the moment someone graduates.
>
> And on her profile, Sarah controls her own story. **Her** goals. **Her** interests. **Her** matching preferences. Academic records stay private. **Privacy by design** — because for Student Affairs, protecting a student's growth is as important as supporting it."

### 重点
- "Not on Discord / disappearing DMs" —— 防止学生关系随毕业流失，对 Student Affairs 同样有力
- **不要**说 "FERPA-compliant by design"（律师话术，对 Student Affairs 不是首要）
- "Privacy by design — protecting growth is as important as supporting it" 呼应 Slide 11 框架

---

## 第 5 段 — 过渡到 Admin Dashboard｜约 20 秒

### 操作
- 地址栏手动输入 `/admin`（但不深入讲细节，留给下一位演讲人）

### 演讲稿
> "So that's Sarah's story. She came in **second-guessing every choice** — quietly **burning out** the way most students do. A few months later — three mentoring relationships, a roadmap she actually believes in, and a major she's chosen *on purpose*. Next semester, she's signing up to be a peer mentor herself. **Paying it forward.**
>
> But Sarah is one of **3,847 active users**. To show you how the platform performs at scale — let me hand it over to my colleague."

### 重点
- "second-guessing every choice" 与 "paying it forward" 都是 Slide 6 对比表原话
- "3,847" 数字要有力
- 最后一句是交接语

## 演示前 Checklist

| 检查项 | 说明 |
|---|---|
| 浏览器 | 提前打开 Landing 页、最大化、关闭无关标签 |
| 网速 | 测试 Vercel 加载速度，备 `npm run dev` 作为 backup |
| 字体 | 确认 Google Fonts（Merriweather, Source Sans 3）正常加载 |
| 鼠标 | 启用 presentation mode 或放大鼠标指针 |
| 语速 | 正常语速刚好，不要赶 |
| 情感 | Sarah 的故事是情感入口，先共情再过渡到数据 |

---

## 叙事逻辑

**先情感，后理性：**
1. 前 3 分多钟让 Student Affairs **感受**到学生的真实痛点（Sarah 是 sophomore，关键词：**burnout / second-guessing / paying-it-forward**，对应 Slide 5–6）
2. 最后 20 秒把情感**转化**为规模化的数据问题
3. Admin Dashboard 用**数据**证明解决方案在规模上成立
